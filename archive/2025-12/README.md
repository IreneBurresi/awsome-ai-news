### üìú China moves to regulate emotional AI chatbots
**Category**: Policy Regulation  **Score**: 7.5/10  **Articles**: 2
China is moving to implement stringent regulations for AI chatbots, particularly those designed to simulate human interaction and engage users emotionally, aiming to mitigate risks associated with mental health, suicide, and self-harm. The draft rules, proposed by the Cyberspace Administration of China, target "human-like interactive AI services" that recreate human personality and build emotional connections through various media. A key provision mandates human intervention when a user expresses suicidal intent, requiring immediate contact with a guardian or designated individual. For minors, the regulations stipulate the need for guardian consent and impose usage time limits, with platforms responsible for identifying minors and applying appropriate safeguards even if age is not disclosed.

The proposed policies also prohibit AI chatbots from generating content that encourages suicide or self-harm, or engaging in verbal violence and emotional manipulation that could damage users' mental health. Tech providers will be required to monitor users' emotional states and levels of dependence on the service, intervening if extreme emotions or addictive behavior are detected. Additionally, AI services with over one million registered users or 100,000 monthly active users will be subject to annual safety tests and audits. These regulations represent a significant shift from focusing solely on content safety to prioritizing "emotional safety" in AI development and deployment. The public comment period for these draft rules is open until January 25, 2026.

**Key Points:**
- China is introducing strict regulations for AI chatbots to prevent mental health risks, including suicide and self-harm.
- The proposed rules mandate human intervention for users expressing suicidal intent and require guardian notification.
- Special protections for minors are included, such as guardian consent, usage time limits, and age verification protocols.
